GRWU: Election Day Edition

Maryland voters, get ready with us for the 2024 election. Here are answers to your most pressing questions.

By Dylan Jaffe, Jackson Hawkins and Viviane Stackhouse

Oct. 15 is the deadline to register to vote, update registration or change party affiliation before the general election. Oct. 15 is the deadline to register to vote, update registration or change party affiliation before the general election.

Yes. In Maryland, you can register to vote during early voting or on election day at the polls.

Early voting begins Oct. 24 from 7 a.m. to 8 p.m. and ends Oct. 31. Residents can vote in any early voting center in the county where they live. Maryland has 97 early voting centers and many counties have multiple early voting locations. You can find more information about early voting centers here.

If you are a first time voter, you may be asked to show a form of identification. You can provide your driver’s license number or state ID card number if you have one. Other forms of ID include: a U.S passport, any state or federal government-issued ID card, a copy of a utility bill, bank statement, or other government issued document that shows your name and current address

Voting takes place on Nov. 5 from 7 a.m. to 8 p.m.

Every voter in Maryland has an assigned voting location where they can vote on election day. Click here to look up your voting location.

To register to vote online, you must use Maryland’s Online Voter Registration System (OLVR). This website will walk you through how to register, but you cannot cast your vote online.

Oct. 29 is the last day to request a mail-in ballot to be delivered via mail or fax, while Nov. 1 at 11:59 p.m. is the last day to request a mail-in ballot via email. Mail-in ballots must be turned in by Nov. 5 at 8 p.m. either via mail, to an approved voter drop box near you or by hand-delivering it to the board of elections.

Yes, all eight congressional districts in the state of Maryland will be voting for a new congressional representative in this election. Look here to find out your district and congressional candidates.

The Democratic candidate is Prince George’s County Executive Angela Alsobrooks, the Republican candidate is Former Maryland Governor Larry Hogan, and the Libertarian candidate is Mike Scott.

Question 1 asks voters to vote “yes” or “no” to the Right to Reproductive Freedom Amendment, which would establish reproductive freedom as a constitutional right in the state of Maryland. Question 1 is a legislatively referred constitutional amendment, which means the Maryland Senate and Maryland House of Representatives have voted to put this question on the ballot. There will be no other voter referendums on your ballot.

According to a recent Pew study, the economy, health care, Supreme Court appointments, foreign policy, violent crimes, immigration, gun policy, abortion, racial and ethnic inequality, and climate change are among the most important issues for voters this year. The majority of voters in this study said that at least five of those ten issues are “very important” to their vote. The economy is the most important issue among all registered voters. Check out the Pew study for more information.
